Nigerian Chicken Stew

Description

In Nigeria, chicken stew can be found in the fridge of almost every house hold. This is because it can be paired with various types of food; spaghetti, white rice, yam (fried or boiled) and much more.

The stew can be made in large quantity and stored in the freezer, this doesnt affect it's freshness. It can be brought out to defrost and microwaved to warm up and enjoy. The amount of ingrdients used is relative to the quatity being cooked.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Garlic and Ginger
  • Bell peppers
  • Scotch bonnet pepper/Atarado
  • Vegetable oil
  • Crayfish
  • Seasonings
  • Scent leaf

Steps

  1. In a blender add the tomatoes, bell peppers, onios, scotch bonnet pepper, garlic and ginger and blend till pureed
  2. Season and boil the chicken, set broth aside.
  3. Add vegetable oil into a pan and fry the boiled chicken to preference and set aside.
  4. Boil tomato mixture till it's dry to reduce the water.
  5. In a pot, add previous vegetable oil and allow to heat up.
  6. Add choppped onions and fry for 1 minute.
  7. Add tomato mixture and allow to fry for 15 to 20 minutes.
  8. Add seasoning.
  9. Add a little chicken stock, mix and allow to fry for 10 minutes.
  10. Add fried chicken and allow to cook for an etra 5 minutes.
